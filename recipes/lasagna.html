Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with rich, savory ingredients. The heart of a traditional lasagna is its hearty meat sauce, typically made from ground beef or pork simmered with onions, garlic, tomatoes, and Italian herbs like basil and oregano. This flavorful sauce is layered between pasta sheets along with creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and plenty of shredded mozzarella, creating a comforting and satisfying meal with a perfect balance of textures and flavors

Baked to golden perfection, lasagna comes out of the oven bubbling and aromatic, with a slightly crispy top layer of cheese. It’s a dish that's perfect for family dinners, gatherings, or meal prepping, as it reheats well and tastes even better the next day. Though it takes some time to prepare, the layers of flavor and the joy it brings to the table make lasagna a beloved favorite in many households around the world.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Olive oil
  • Onion, chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Dried basil
  • Dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Egg
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh parsley (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil and sauté chopped onions and minced garlic until soft.
  4. Add ground beef or sausage to the skillet and cook until browned. Drain excess fat if needed.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  6. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, grated Parmesan, and chopped parsley (if using).
  7. Spread a layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Add a layer of lasagna noodles, then spread some ricotta mixture, followed by shredded mozzarella.
  9. Repeat the layers (sauce, noodles, ricotta, mozzarella) until the ingredients are used up.
  10. Finish with a generous layer of meat sauce and mozzarella cheese on top.
  11. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  12. Remove foil and bake uncovered for an additional 15–20 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  13. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
