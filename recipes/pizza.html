Italian Pizza

Description

The Italian Margherita Pizza is a classic Neapolitan pizza known for its simple yet flavorful combination of ingredients. It features a thin, chewy crust topped with fresh tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella, fragrant basil leaves, and a drizzle of olive oil. This pizza represents the colors of the Italian flag — red, white, and green — and is celebrated for its authenticity and elegance.

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough ball (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce (preferably San Marzano tomatoes)
  • 150g fresh mozzarella (Fior di Latte or Buffalo Mozzarella)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1–2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Flour for dusting

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to its highest setting (around 250°C / 480°F). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while preheating.
  2. Lightly flour your work surface and stretch or roll out the dough into a thin round shape.
  3. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  4. Tear the mozzarella into small chunks and distribute it over the sauce.
  5. Add a few fresh basil leaves on top.
  6. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil and add a pinch of salt.
  7. Transfer the pizza to the preheated oven (or pizza stone) and bake for 7–10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.
  8. Remove from the oven, add more fresh basil if desired, slice, and serve hot.
